Why should you stop doing things just for likes?

Hi there! How is the creative process going?

 Did you stop doing those things you like because there are no external reactions? No like, no comments? Hear me out: S T O P ! Stop doing that, babe.

For those who are starting a new project or trying something
new, mainly in the creative realm, it is tempting to test proved it with how
many likes it gets. If I reach a particular response ( 100’s of likes or maybe 1000’s), it means I am doing great; if not, it is a direct ticket to “I am a failure Town”. 

But why should you stop doing things just for likes? Well:

TagsTagsBlogCreative ProcessDate04-05-2020














There’s no improvement without failure

You have decided to go out there and exhibit your so new creative
project, but it got cero response once out there.  What is your first reaction? Of course, it would be to think that there’s something you are doing wrong, so you stop. And that, my friend, is the mistake. We prevent creating any improvement by quitting because our project didn’t have a good response.  

You won’t be able to be better and achieve greatness if you don’t practice every single day, with or without likes.

So if you need a sign, this is it: Do things because you
LIKE /LOVE them, no just for likes.

Do things because you LIKE /LOVE them, no just for likes.

When we engage in activities that we are passionate about, good
things come out of it, and we will find ways to improve them. So next time your post, art, or project don’t get the response you are expecting, think about how I can do this better for myself? Fall in love with it and keep trying and evolving.

No one reaches mastery by quitting every time you don’t get
the response you expected. So keep pushing, keep trying!

Unfortunately, we all fall to compare with others, big thanks
to social media, where we can see other artists getting tons of likes by doing what they like. Which can add to our feeling of: Why should I even try? But you have to remember; it is a journey, and you have just started it. So, don’t compare yourself with those who have been there for years or have practiced every single day. For instance, don’t compare yourself with anyone, and focus on practice and improvement. Do things and continue your journey because only actions will get results.

So, do things because you love doing them, do them for you.
Likes and followers will come soon after.

Have an amazing day,

Karin Polanco
